The Agile Manifesto is comprised of 4 key values, and 12 principles,
extablished by 17 Agile Alliance developers in February, 2011 (Rouse, 2015).

The Agile process was established to improve upon the methodologies
in software development. The traditional way prior to agile, accoding to the
was "cumbersome, unresponsive, and too much focus on documentation
requirements". There are various approaches to agile, one we will focus on is
called scrum.

The traditional way of going about software development
utilized the trickle down approach, where work is distributed in a team like
a sequenced relay. This is an ideal approach when the workflow is predictable.

Agile is advantageous when there are roadblocks in the process, where many
revisions are expected.
